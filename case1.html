This particular case study will go through the process of solving the problem of Solent student app known as “MySolent”. The process of solving this problem has been inspired from Sprint Design book

. . .

Problem statement:

In every new academic year, students are being advised to use the student app in order to simplify their life at Solent university and take advantage of many facilities designated to help them succeed in their studies. However, a number of students get frustrated by the low performance, quality, and design of the app. Therefore, the majority of students eventually choose to use the web alternatives, such as SOL and Student Portal.

The number of students who use smartphones at Solent is significant and cannot be ignored. Thus, it is important to focus on this category of users and update the current app in the form of a mobile application that can be used by students on a daily basis throughout their studies at Solent university.

This should provide students with access to all the essential facilities and services to make their life at Solent university smooth and easy — All in one mobile application.

Research & Insight

Based on the analysis of students reviews on the two platforms iOS and Android , it seems the app has a number of issues in terms of the information architect, the usability, and the data response.

The analysis of students reviews helped not only to get accurate information from the main users, but also understand the problem they face while using the app throughout their studies at Solent university.

The reviews have shown students’ desire to enhance and elevate the usability of the app on the two platforms. And most importantly, students hope to make this app become a comprehensive alternative to all the websites provided by Solent university.

Main learnings:
  • Students don’t feel the app meets today’s design standards.
  • Students are frustrated by the long time of loading and data response.
  • Students don’t like to see the app features as a mirroring to the web alternatives site. Example: Student Calendar.
  • More than half of the students do not use the app.
Quotes:
  • “MySolent doesn’t work properly, and it’s very non-user-friendly”
  • “Honestly, you’re better off accessing sol on desktop”
  • “The app looks like Windows Phone interface”
  • “The app needs a dark theme”
  • “The app constantly being hit with an error when trying to access different parts of the app”

Low-Fidelity Designs

A number of wireframes have been designed to try and explore different ideas and design solutions in order to create the best experience possible.

The focus has been given to the creation of a new design experience based on the analysis generated from students reviews as well as based on today’s design standards in terms of usability, responsiveness, and clarity.

In addition to this, a big focus has been given to the primary and secondary sections of the app that are considered as the most sections used by students on a daily basis throughout their studies at Solent University.

In this wire flow, the tasks of the app have been summarized in eight tasks:
the first four tasks have been placed in the primary sections, whereas the second four tasks in the secondary section in the bottom navigation.

The first four tasks in the primary sections (Main tabs):
  1. Timetable: Check your time table — Select a Lesson — Read lesson brief, time, and get direction to the class location in the navigation tab.
  2. Email: Check uni email inbox — check the selected email and the choose the reply options.
  3. Tasks: Check your upcoming task in the list of tasks — Click read more to get extra details on the SOL page
  4. Navigation: By default, the upcoming class map will be presented on the map section. Plus, an option to get the destination of the classrooms from the timetable section.
The second four tasks in the primary sections (Main tabs):
  1. Home: Check your time table — Select a Lesson — Read lesson brief, time, and get direction to the class location in the navigation tab.
  2. SOL: Check your module brief from the list of your course by clicking read more to get module details on SOL page on the app.
  3. Student Portal: Choose from the six options available on Portal (Attendance — Exam results — Library Service — Mental health support — Student Union — Terms Dates)
  4. Library: Search for any book in the library catalogue — Preview the book sample — get an option to borrow the hard version or use the digital copy.

The aim behind this wire flow process is to formulate and explore various ideas and design solutions of many tasks on the app before getting into the steps of the high fidelity designs.

High-Fidelity Designs

These are the first samples of the high fidelity design appearance. The aim was to create a simple and minimal user interface design inspired from Material design system. And most importantly, a user interface design that will not consume students’ mobile internet.

The colour palette has been inspired from Solent university brand identity in order to create a user interface that is familiarized by the students in terms of the visual appearance and design elements similar to the other platforms provided by the university such as SOL and Student Portal.

A number of familiar design components have been used in the new design in order to be recognized and simply understood by the users. This new design interface is expected to give the app only a fresh look , but also a new usability experience.

. . .

RESULTS

Many students who have tested the app expressed their positive feedback on the new information architect and user interface as a new usability experience to interact with the app sections.

However, the aim is to integrate more services and enhance several sections of the app such as the map and the library with a view to facilitate the usage and give more convenient experience. This is expected to positively impact students’ experience throughout their studies at Solent University.

Another aspect is the top header, which some students suggest denominating it to promote university events, news, and offers that completely matches with the thoughts we have already stetted for this section in the site-mapping phase.

Moreover, students have expressed their desire to add a direct chatting experience with the classmate or tutors on the app in which they’ll be able to communicate and collaborate together without relying on an applications outside the Student app.

Overall, the usability has been improved compared with the old design, although some important improvements will be added to the app soon, particularly on the map section with more A.R experience.
